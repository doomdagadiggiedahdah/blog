Homeomorphism (big boi)

When we take one map and change it to another (‚Äúsame‚Äù, but ‚Äúdifferent‚Äù) map, that‚Äôs called a ‚Äúhomeomorphism‚Äù. This can roughly be broken into:

  • homeo = ‚Äúsame‚Äù
  • morphism = ‚Äúshape, form, structure‚Äù

‚ÄúSame structure‚Äù == they have the same connections.

We could talk about homeomorphisms by saying ‚ÄúMap 1 and 2 are homeomorphic to each other‚Äù or ‚ÄúMap 2 is a homeomorphic transformation of Map 1‚Äù. And we can say this for all four maps, they‚Äôre all homeomorphic to each other. They‚Äôre all ‚Äúthe same‚Äù in some way (same connections!), but they‚Äôre also all ‚Äúdifferent‚Äù from each other (different shapes and details and stuff).

Invariant:

‚ÄúSame structure‚Äù, but some things are different‚Ä¶but some things are the same? Yes! and those things that stay the same are the ‚Äúinvariants‚Äù because they‚Ä¶don‚Äôt vary. MATHEMATICIAN YOU ARE! Again, in the case of these maps, what‚Äôs not changing are the connections or the connectivity of said maps. In every map you can get from North Berkeley to Downtown Berkeley, Downtown Berkeley to Union City, Union City to Balboa Park. Since those connections are the same in every map, we can use every map to plan our travel. Invariant!!
